# Workflow Economics — Revenue Intelligence Framework

Board-ready four-tab framework for evaluating Revenue Intelligence as **revenue amplification**, not labor displacement.

## Purpose

This artifact helps CRO, CIO, CFO, and board audiences evaluate Revenue Intelligence programs through a capital-allocation lens.

The core distinction:

```text
Cost-side AI displaces work.
Revenue Intelligence amplifies revenue.
```

That means the relevant investment test is not break-even. The model asks whether a workflow clears a WACC-adjusted ROIC hurdle.

## Live Version

https://grikard.github.io/revenue-intelligence-framework/

## Framework Structure

The artifact includes four panels:

1. **Amplification Equation**  
   Defines Revenue Intelligence as fidelity × revenue opportunity over program cost plus misguidance penalty.

2. **Three Scaling Regimes**  
   Compares token cost efficiency, GPU AI performance, and Moore's Law.

3. **Decision Matrix**  
   Maps representative workflows across four capital postures, with industry-specific views.

4. **Workflow Assessor**  
   Scores a workflow across judgment complexity, error consequence, AI capability today, and unit economics today.

## Core Formula

```text
Amplification Ratio =
(Fidelity × Revenue Opportunity)
÷
(Program Cost + Misguidance Penalty)
```

Revenue-side AI should clear a hurdle above break-even:

```text
Amplification Ratio > WACC-adjusted ROIC hurdle
```

The artifact uses:

```text
2.0×
```

as the default executive hurdle.

## Four Capital Allocation Postures

| Posture | Typical Horizon | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| **Automate Now** | 0–6 months | Workflow economics already clear the hurdle |
| **Sequence Next** | 12–24 months | Prepare infrastructure before capability catches up |
| **Build & Defend** | 24–48 months | Invest in proprietary signal, semantic integration, and data moat |
| **Protect & Invest** | 36+ months | Preserve human judgment and deepen the strategic data substrate |
